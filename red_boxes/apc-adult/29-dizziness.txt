Give urgent attention to the patient with dizziness and any of:
• New sudden asymmetric weakness or numbness of face, arm or leg; difficulty
speaking or visual disturbance: consider stroke or TIA 136.
• BP < 90/60
• Pulse < 50 or irregular
• Glucose < 3 (or < 4 if diabetes)  17
• Difficulty breathing, especially on lying flat with leg swelling 135
• Recent head injury
• Unable to stand without support
• New sudden severe dizziness with nausea/vomiting, abnormal eye
movements or walk
• Chest pain 37
Manage and refer urgently:
• If BP < 90/60, give sodium chloride 0.9% 500mL IV over 30 minutes, repeat until systolic BP > 90. Continue 1L 6 hourly. Stop if breathing worsens.
